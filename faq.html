Guard Card FAQ

Ask now. Not after you paid.

Every question people call Lou with, answered straight. On licensing matters the state always gets the last word — DESPP Special Licensing & Firearms Unit, (860) 685-8046.

What do I need to become a licensed security officer in CT?

Three things: complete the state-mandated 8-hour Security Officer Certification class (that's the class Lou teaches), meet the state requirements — at least 18 years old, no felony or sexual-offense convictions, and U.S. citizen or resident alien — and then file your application with the state.

You leave our class with the signed application form (DPS-135-C) and everything needed to file.

How much does the whole thing cost, start to finish?

The class itself, plus the state's filing costs. The state currently charges an application fee ($100), an FBI background inquiry ($12), a State of Connecticut background inquiry ($75), and fingerprint card costs — paid by separate check or money order to the Treasurer, State of Connecticut. Fingerprinting for our students is $25.

State fees change from time to time — confirm current amounts with DESPP when you file.

How long is my Guard Card good for? What about renewal?

Your card is good for 5 years. Renewal is currently $100 every 5 years — and as long as you never let the card expire, you never have to take the class again.

Important: if your card expires for more than 90 days, the state treats you as a brand-new applicant — class, fees, everything, from scratch. Put the renewal date in your phone the day you get your card.

How long is my class certificate valid before I file?

Your certificate from the class is good for up to one year. File within that window or the state will require you to take the course again. Our advice: file the same week you take the class — you'll have everything you need in your folder.

I have a security license from New York or another state. Does it transfer?

No. Connecticut does not accept security officer certifications from any other state. You'll need to take the CT class and obtain the Connecticut Guard Card.

I'm a veteran / active military / former law enforcement. Do I still need the class?

Maybe not. Under Public Act 14-131, the state currently waives the course for veterans honorably discharged within 2 years, active military, reserves, and National Guard — and for some, not all, retired law enforcement officers, on a case-by-case basis.

Before registering, call the Special Licensing & Firearms Unit at (860) 685-8046 — only the state can grant the waiver. Corrections officers are not exempt and must take the class.

What does it take to work armed?

Armed security is a second layer on top of your Guard Card. You must be at least 21, hold a CT State Pistol Permit, and complete the state-approved firearms course for the Blue Card endorsement (form DPS-1030, currently a $62 state fee). The endorsement is good for 5 years, runs parallel with your pistol permit, and requires an annual firearms refresher.

We teach every step of that path — see the Armed Officer Track on our Programs & Pricing page.

Where do I file, and how long until I can work?

Applications go to the Department of Emergency Services and Public Protection, Special Licensing & Firearms Unit, 1111 Country Club Road, Middletown, CT 06457. Your class folder includes directions and every form.

Many employers will hire you with your certification and a pending application on file with DESPP — which is why some of our graduates are working within a couple of weeks of class.

Do bouncers need a Guard Card?

It depends on the role and how the venue employs its security staff — Connecticut's rules turn on whether you're performing security officer services. Call us at (203) 907-6594 and describe the job; we'll tell you straight whether you need the card.

What if I lose my certificate or my card?

For a lost class certificate, contact us about a duplicate. For a lost state card, that's handled by DESPP — call (860) 685-8046 and they'll walk you through replacement.

Licensing rules and fees are set by the State of Connecticut and can change. Verify current requirements with DESPP SLFU: (860) 685-8046 · DESPP.SLFU@ct.gov
