About LJB

The instructor is the product

Anyone can rent a classroom and read the state curriculum out loud. LJB exists because who teaches you — and what they've actually done — changes what you walk out with.

[ Photo: Lou teaching a class ]
Lou Bonito

Twenty years inside. Eleven years teaching.

Lou spent two decades in Connecticut Corrections managing aggressive behavior, de-escalation, restraint, and arrest techniques in the hardest classroom there is. He holds over 100 security-related certifications and has trained more than 15,000 licensed officers — about 7 of every 10 Guard Cards in the state.

What makes an LJB class different isn't the curriculum — the state sets that. It's everything around it: the employer hiring list Lou maintains personally, the paperwork help before you leave the room, the job board updated daily, and a straight answer to every question. You tell him where you want to end up; he tells you the shortest route there.

The LJB Story

Built on one law and a lot of reps

2004–2008

Connecticut mandates the profession

The state passes legislation requiring training and licensing for uniformed security officers — Public Act 08-73 — with all private security training approved and all instructors certified by DESPP.

2012

LJB Security Training opens

Lou brings 20 years of Corrections experience to the classroom and starts teaching the mandated 8-hour course out of East Haven.

2012–Today

The busiest guard school in the state

15,000+ graduates. The full catalog grows to 20+ courses — defensive tactics, CPR, armed certification, leadership seminars, and organizational training. Security companies begin hiring directly out of class.

Now

Training & consulting for an ever-changing world

Beyond individual licensing: corporate accounts, security consulting, workplace violence certification, and team training for churches, schools, healthcare, and business.

Consulting & Corporate

It's not just a school

LJB also works directly with organizations — site security consulting, corporate training accounts, and staff-wide certification programs. If you're responsible for a building, a congregation, or a workforce, start with the team tracks on our Programs page or call Lou directly.
